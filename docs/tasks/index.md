## How to Use These Tasks

1. **Read the problem** — understand the requirements and constraints fully before writing any code.
2. **Try it yourself** — spend at least 15–30 minutes on each task before looking at the solution.
3. **Check the hints** — if stuck, read one hint at a time.
4. **Compare your solution** — even if yours works, study the suggested solution for idioms and edge-case handling.
5. **Explain it aloud** — practice talking through your approach as you would in an interview.

## Difficulty Legend

- **Easy** — straightforward, single-concept problem (warm-up)
- **Medium** — requires combining 2–3 concepts or careful edge-case handling
- **Hard** — open-ended design, performance constraints, or multiple valid trade-offs
